Envisioning the Future of Plate Reader Applications for Clinical & Research Labs

product-design

secondary-research

usability-study

contextual-inquiry

This project aims to improve the experience of plate reader applications used in life sciences and diagnostics. Current products on the market are known to have poor usability with outdated and inconsistent interfaces, along with constant information overload. The goal is to create a flexible and scalable product that can work for any plate reader protocol.

Building a Custom Design System for Medical Applications

ui-design

secondary-research

interaction-design

This project is an ongoing effort at Revvity to create one source of truth for all product design in the company, encompassing software, hardware, and reagents. Efforts have been underway for multiple years, but the beginning of a scalable and cohesive design system with documentation began in 2022, during my first internship at the company. I played a key role in getting this project up-and-running, and designed many components found in the system ranging from small 'atoms' (button, text field, file upload) to larger 'systems' (log in, reset password, system status).
