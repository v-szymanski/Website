PERKINELMER & REVVITY INTERNSHIPS

Building a Custom Design System for Medical Applications

Overview

This project is an ongoing effort at Revvity to establish a single source of truth for all product design, including software, hardware, and reagents. Initiated in 2022 during my first internship, I helped evolve the design system into a scalable and company-wide project involving the design team, senior leaders, developers, and product managers. I played a key role in launching the project and designed various components, ranging from small 'atoms' like buttons and text fields to larger 'systems' like log-in and system status. Then, as the company underwent a divestiture and became known as Revvity, I helped define a new branding strategy for product design and built some of the first components under the new guidelines. The public documentation website is available at revvity.design.

Background

Context

Revvity (previously known as PerkinElmer) is a major producer of biotechnology products. It creates software and hardware solutions to serve scientists, researchers, and clinicians in the life sciences and diagnostics industries. I contributed to the creation and, later, the rebranding of the company wide design system. Follow my journey from January 2022 to December 2023!

Timeline

My work on this project spans over 3 co-op terms that I spent at Revvity (previously affiliated with PerkinElmer), as it evolved from a lofty aspiration into a highly successful initiative with buy-in and support from teams across the company. During my first term with the company (January to April 2022), I helped get the project off the ground by compiling tons of previous work from designers before me, revamping a documentation website to ensure our progress was accessible to other teams, and advocating for the systemâ€™s growth. My second term (September to December 2022) was dedicated to building 10+ new components, streamlining our feedback & documentation process, and improving consistency across the design system. Finally, the design system underwent a massive overhaul in my final term (September to December 2023) due to a divestiture that gave us a new company name, Revvity, and saw a complete 180 in terms of branding guidelines. In my final four months, I helped define a new branding strategy for product design, built some of the first components using our updated design language, and helped multiple teams rebrand their products.
